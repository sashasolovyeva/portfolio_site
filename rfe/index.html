Joining the amazing visual journalism team of Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty, I was very eager to pitch my own publication. After my idea of an inforgraphic about Alexei Navalny's detainment timeline was approved by the leading editor of the team, I dived deep into media research to assemble the complete sequence of events. We ended up compiling our own list by looking at news reports by Russian and international media, as well as through Navalny's own Instagram and Twitter feeds.

I was then trained in basics of Adobe Illustrator on the spot to help with assembling the infographic.

This is the first detailed documentation of all of Navalny's arrests (as of Fall 2018) published. It was retweeted by Alexei Navalny.

My first day as an intern at RFE/RL was marked by a big news story: the Ukrainian Orthodox Church had become independent, signifying yet another decline in Ukraine's relationship with Russia. Due to the complicated structure of the Orthodox Church, however, the significance of the event wasn't readily obvious to the general public. I teamed up with my colleague in the visual journalism team to help her with the robust amount of historical, cultural and linguistic research that needed to be done on the topic.
